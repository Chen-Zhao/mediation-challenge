Baron and Kenny (B&K) with multiple mediators

2 steps approach described in Kupers et al. 2015

  • EWAS markers: Finds markers correlated with exposure (FDR\(<5\%\) using Bonferroni correction in Step 2)
  • Baron and Kenny procedure (\(P<0.05\) in Step 2 and Step 3)

Results

EWAS_m<-(myp_step2<0.05/nb_med)
candidate<-names(data)[EWAS_m &(myp_step3<0.05) & (myp_step2<0.05)]
## Baron and Kenny procedure 
##  power:  0.3666667  FDR:  0.4054054

The procedure is too liberal because Baron and Kenny procedure does not account for multiple testing. In this example, Baron and Kenny procedure will be applied 339 times, which is the number of EWAS markers.

Sobel test

Should not use p-values from the Sobel test because they are poorly calibrated. The test will be too conservative.

Sobel test

candidate_sobel<-names(data)[ myp_sobel<(0.05/nb_med)]
## Sobel only 
##  power:  0.1  FDR:  0

Sobel test AND FDRTOOLS

Recalibration of P-values

require(fdrtool)
mynewp_sobel<-fdrtool(z_sobel,plot=FALSE)$pval
## Step 1... determine cutoff point
## Step 2... estimate parameters of null distribution and eta0
## Step 3... compute p-values and estimate empirical PDF/CDF
## Step 4... compute q-values and local fdr

Sobel test AND FDRTOOLS

P-values are properly calibrated.

hist(mynewp_sobel,breaks=50)

Results obtained with Sobel test AND FDRTOOLS

candidate_sobel<-names(data)[mynewp_sobel<(0.05/nb_med)]
## Sobel and fdrtool 
##  power:  0.3333333  FDR:  0.1666667

Less conservative that Sobel test with no P-values calibration.

Conclusion

–Check distribution of P-values when performing multiple testing.

–fdrtool is a useful package to recalibrate P-values using empirical null distribution.
